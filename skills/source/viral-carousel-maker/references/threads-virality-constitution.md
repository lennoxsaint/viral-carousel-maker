# Threads Virality Constitution

This skill optimizes against observed Threads mechanics. It never guarantees virality, but it must protect the user from predictable patterns that make carousel posts feel generic, needy, or overbuilt.

## Seven corpus laws

1. No how-to hooks by default.
   Top-performing Threads posts usually open with observations, contradictions, confessions, identity mirrors, or proof-first receipts. Use how-to hooks only when the user explicitly wants search-oriented educational content.

2. Compress until the sentence feels certain.
   Threads rewards short, clear, confident language. Cut throat-clearing, setup phrases, generic adjectives, and AI-ish framing before rendering.

3. Default to fewer body slides for reach.
   Use 3 body slides for reach mode, 5 for saveable education, and 7 or 9 only when the carousel has story, proof, examples, or raw lived stakes.

4. Delete fake urgency.
   Ban urgency sprinkles, generic transformation bait, and "growth journey" sludge unless the user has real proof that earns the claim.

5. Treat CTAs as a reach tax.
   Every CTA must be earned. Prefer low-pressure CTAs unless the carousel is explicitly built for conversion and the value is strong enough.

6. Avoid homework questions.
   Questions often make the reader work. Prefer statements, identity mirrors, polarizing flags, and recognition sentences.

7. Long content needs humanity.
   Long/dense carousels must include raw lived stakes, proof, story, or receipts. Information alone is not enough.

## Enforcement

Before rendering:

- Generate at least 5 hook candidates.
- Score the selected hook against `hook-lab.md`.
- Name the old belief and new belief.
- Pick the minimum body-slide count that can carry the point.
- Choose CTA pressure deliberately: none, soft, medium, or hard.
- Write one `visual_thesis`: mood, material, energy, and dominant visual idea.

During QA:

- Reject banned hook openers.
- Reject homework-question hooks unless they are true identity mirrors.
- Reject overlong hook copy.
- Reject over-dense body slides.
- Reject unearned high-pressure CTAs.
- Reject first slides that look visually weak, generic, or cluttered.
