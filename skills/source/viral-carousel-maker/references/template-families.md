# Template families

Use one family per carousel. Vary modules inside the family, not the overall visual system.

## 1. Story
Best for personal transformation, founder lessons, and before/after arcs.

## 2. List
Best for "X things", tactical tips, and broad saveable posts.

## 3. Framework
Best for named systems, mental models, and repeatable processes.

## 4. Timeline
Best for progressions, roadmaps, maturity curves, and "from this to that" posts.

## 5. Debate
Best for myth vs truth, bad vs viral, and opinionated comparison posts.

## 6. Examples
Best for swipe files, before/after copy, teardown examples, and pattern libraries.

## 7. Data
Best for stats, benchmark findings, quantified proof, and ranked takeaways.

## 8. Quote
Best for one powerful idea expanded into lessons.

## 9. Map
Best for ecosystems, workflows, diagrams, and tool stacks.

## 10. Mistakes
Best for "stop doing this", anti-patterns, and course-correction posts.

## 11. Recap
Best for short summaries, cheat sheets, and dense reference cards.

## 12. CTA
Best for offer-led carousels where the core value leads naturally into action.

## Reference-Inspired Family Notes

Use this as one visual family only:

- Textured white paper
- Large condensed headlines
- Navy text
- Orange accent rules and arrows
- Optional pink/green contrast accents
- Body progress markers like `1/5`
- Simple diagram logic
- Big whitespace
- Bottom-left handle

Do not copy exact reference layouts. Extract spacing, hierarchy, and pacing.

## Visual modes

Template family decides the content mechanic. `visual_mode` decides the slide art direction.

Use these modes across families:

- `shock-stat`: best for data, proof, and contrarian hooks.
- `proof-grid`: best for receipts, metrics, examples, and proof stacks.
- `myth-truth`: best for debate, mistakes, and belief shifts.
- `taxonomy`: best for map, framework, and category breakdowns.
- `quiet-truth`: best for emotional, premium, or reflective slides.
- `receipt`: best for research notes, audits, and proof-backed claims.
- `contrast-table`: best for bad/good, before/after, and old/new belief.
- `field-note`: best for founder observations and lived-experience insights.
- `photo-anchor`: best when one generated image/object should carry the story.

Default pairings:

- Reach + shock stat: `data` or `debate` family with `shock-stat`.
- Identity challenge: `mistakes` or `debate` with `quiet-truth`.
- Myth teardown: `debate` with `myth-truth` or `contrast-table`.
- Proof carousel: `data` or `examples` with `proof-grid` and `receipt`.
- Soft CTA education: `framework` or `list` with `editorial-paper`.
